# Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) Algorithm Approach

## Overview
Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) refers to cryptographic algorithms that are designed to be secure against potential attacks from quantum computers. The advent of quantum computing poses a significant threat to traditional cryptographic algorithms, such as RSA and ECC, which could be easily broken by quantum algorithms like Shor's algorithm.

## Key Concepts
1. **Quantum Resistance**: PQC algorithms must be secure against both classical and quantum attacks.
   - They leverage mathematical problems that are currently considered hard for quantum computers, such as the Learning With Errors (LWE) problem or the Shortest Vector Problem (SVP).

2. **Diversity**: Multiple families of algorithms are being studied, including lattice-based, code-based, multivariate polynomial, and hash-based cryptography. This diversity is crucial for building a robust cryptographic infrastructure that can withstand unforeseen advances in quantum computing.

## Conclusion
The development of post-quantum algorithms is crucial for securing our digital future. Continuous research and rigorous testing will drive the evolution of these algorithms to ensure they remain secure as technology advances.
